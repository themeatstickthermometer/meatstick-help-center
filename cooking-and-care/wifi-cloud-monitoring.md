# Monitoring your cook over WiFi

WiFi is what lets you check a cook from the grocery store, the office, or bed during an overnight smoke — not just from the backyard. Here's how it works on the V Series.

## Where the WiFi lives

WiFi is built into the **base or hub**, not the Stick. The Stick talks to the base over Bluetooth; the base connects to your home WiFi and streams the cook to the MeatStick Cloud. From there it reaches your phone anywhere with internet.

That means cloud monitoring is available on the sets with a WiFi-capable base:

* **V Prime Set** — V Smart Base
* **V Duo Set** — V Duo Smart Base
* **V Forge Set** — V WiFi Hub

The **V Core Set** is Bluetooth-only (phone-direct), so it doesn't do cloud monitoring on its own. Add it to a set with a WiFi base, or step up to one of the above.

## Setting it up

1. In the MeatStick App, tap **Add device** and put your base in pairing mode.
2. Pick your WiFi network and enter the password.
3. The base joins WiFi and starts sending cook data to the cloud.

> **2.4 GHz only.** The base connects to **2.4 GHz** networks, not 5 GHz. Most home routers broadcast both on the same name — if pairing won't find your network, put your phone on the 2.4 GHz band during setup. Once paired, the base remembers the network; you won't re-pair every cook.

No dongles, no separate gateway, and no monthly subscription — cloud monitoring is included.

For more on the cloud and the app side, see [What is The MeatStick Cloud](../app/what-is-the-meatstick-cloud-and-how-to-use-it.md) and [What is WiFi Link](../app/what-is-wifi-link-and-why-is-it-important.md).

## If your WiFi drops mid-cook

You don't lose the cook. The base keeps streaming temperature to your phone over Bluetooth whenever the phone is in range. You only lose the *remote* window until WiFi comes back. To keep the Bluetooth link solid, keep the base within about 6 feet of the Stick — see [Range and connection drops](../troubleshooting/range-and-connection-drops.md).
