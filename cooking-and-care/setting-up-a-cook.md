# Setting up a cook

Once your Stick is registered, starting a cook takes about 20 seconds. Tap **Start New Cook** and pick one of two ways to go.

## Two ways to set a target

| | **Guided** | **Quick Start** |
|---|---|---|
| How it works | Tell the app what you're cooking — it fills in the target temperature for you | You type in your own target temperature |
| Best for | "I'm not sure what temp it should hit" | You already know your number |

## The Guided flow

1. **How are you cooking?** — Grill, Oven, Smoke, Sous Vide, Stove Top, Deep Fry, or Air Fryer.
2. **What are you cooking?** — Beef, Pork, Poultry, Seafood, or Lamb.
3. **How done?** — Rare, Medium-Rare, Medium, Medium-Well, or Well-Done (the options depend on the cut).
4. The app **fills in a safe target temperature** for that choice. You can fine-tune it if you like.
5. Optionally set your **cooker temperature** and a **name** for the cook, then tap **Start Cook**.

## The two temperatures you'll see

- **Internal temperature** — the meat itself. This is the number that decides when it's done.
- **Ambient temperature** — the cooking environment (your oven, grill, or smoker). On a V Prime Stick this comes from the Stick's built-in sensor; on a V Forge it comes from the Wired Ambient Probe at the grate.

## You can change anything mid-cook

Target temp, alerts, and the cook's name are all editable while you're cooking — as long as your Stick is connected (over Bluetooth or WiFi). They lock only when you're viewing remotely over the cloud.
